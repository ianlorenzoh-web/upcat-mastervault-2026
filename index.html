🗺️ UPCAT Process Guide

Complete, step-by-step guide to the UPCAT — from eligibility to results. Your roadmap to becoming an Isko or Iska.

🏛️
University of the Philippines
College Admission Test (UPCAT)

The UPCAT is the college admission test for all UP System campuses. It tests Language Proficiency, Reading Comprehension, Mathematics, and Science. Your score combines with your High School GWA to produce your University Predicted Grade (UPG) — the lower, the better.

~100,000+ takers/year
~14% passing rate
4 subtests · ~5 hours
Held annually (Aug 1–2, 2026)
— Step-by-Step Application Process
01
Check Eligibility
For UPCAT 2027 (Academic Year 2027–2028), you must be a current Grade 11 student (SY 2025–2026) who will graduate from senior high school in 2027. You can only take the UPCAT once in your lifetime — there are no retakes.
02
Fill Out Form 1 (Application Period: March 3–30, 2026)
Go to the official UPCAT Online Application System (OAS) at upcat.up.edu.ph and fill out Form 1 — the Application for First Year Admission Personal Data Sheet. Use an active email address you check regularly; all notifications will be sent there.
03
School Confirmation via Form 2A (Deadline: April 10, 2026)
The online portal automatically forwards a request to your high school principal or school head to fill out school information and confirm your enrollment or graduation status. Your high school then submits Form 2A (Senior High School Profile) and confirms that you are currently enrolled in Grade 11 or have already graduated from senior high school.
04
Pay the Application Fee
Pay the UPCAT application fee. The application fee is free for Filipino applicants. Check the official UP Admissions site for the most current payment details.
05
Await Your Test Permit (Release: July 2026)
Details regarding the availability and release of the UPCAT 2027 test permit will be sent via email once available. Test permits are expected to be released in July 2026. Log in to your OAS account to download it when notified — print it on white bond paper and do not resize or alter it.
06
Take the UPCAT (August 1–2, 2026)
The UPCAT 2027 will be held on August 1 and 2, 2026. Arrive at your assigned test center at least 30 minutes early. Bring your printed test permit, at least 2 valid IDs, #2 or HB pencils, a black ballpen, and an eraser. No calculators, phones, smartwatches, or electronic devices are allowed. Listen carefully to the proctor's instructions before starting.
07
Submit Form 2B — Grades Submission (August 3–24, 2026)
After the exam, update and resubmit Form 1 (Personal Data Sheet), then fill out and submit Form 2B (Secondary School Record). The online portal will notify your high school regarding access to the Form 2A Portal and request a certified true copy of the Permanent Secondary School Record (F137/SF10). Your high school then submits Form 2A and confirms you are currently enrolled in Grade 12 or have already graduated from senior high school.
08
Official School Record Validation
The Office of Admissions receives a hard copy of your official school record, subject to validation. Once available in the portal, download a PDF copy of your submitted Forms 1 and 2B, as well as the acknowledgment receipt.
09
Wait for Results
UPCAT results are typically released 4–6 months after the exam (usually January–April of the following year). Results are announced on the official UP Admissions website and individual campus websites. Check regularly and use your OAS login credentials to view your results. Passers will receive instructions for enrollment.
— Eligibility & Qualification Requirements
🎓
Who Can Apply for UPCAT?
  • Current Grade 11 students (SY 2025–2026) who will graduate from senior high school in 2027 are the primary applicants for UPCAT 2027 (Academic Year 2027–2028).
  • Recent SHS graduates who completed senior high school in the immediately preceding school year — subject to specific UP guidelines each year.
  • Applicants must not have previously taken the UPCAT — you only get one chance.
  • Applicants must meet the minimum GWA requirement set by UP (typically at least 83 or 84 average in core subjects — verify with the current application bulletin).
  • ALS passers who meet the equivalent academic requirements may also be eligible — check the official announcement each cycle.
📄
Documents Required for Application
  • ID Photo: Recent 1x1 or 2x2 photo on white background, formal attire, with name tag. Digital upload required.
  • Grade 10 Report Card (Form 138): Shows your cumulative grades from Junior High School. This is used to compute your initial GWA for the application.
  • Certificate of Enrollment: Proves you are currently enrolled in Grade 11 during the application period.
  • Form 2B (Secondary School Record): Submitted after the exam (August 3–24, 2026). This records your official school grades and is submitted together with your updated Form 1.
  • For graduates: Certified true copy of the Permanent Secondary School Record (F137/SF10) — submitted by the school to the Office of Admissions for validation.
— Choosing Your Campus & Degree Program
🏫
Quota vs Non-Quota Programs — What's the Difference?

Quota programs have a fixed number of available slots per campus. Admission is purely competitive — only the applicants with the lowest UPG within the quota are admitted. Even a passing UPG may not be enough if the quota is already filled by students with lower UPGs.

Non-quota programs admit all applicants who pass — there is no slot limit. Any applicant who achieves a UPG at or below the program's passing threshold is admitted.

  • Quota programs include: Medicine (pre-med), Engineering, Architecture, Fine Arts, Nursing, Physical Therapy, and several other competitive courses. Check the current bulletin for the official list.
  • Non-quota programs include: Education, Social Sciences, Humanities, and other liberal arts-type programs.
  • Applicants are ranked by UPG within quota programs — the lower your UPG, the more competitive you are.
  • Strategize your program choices: put your most desired program first, but include at least one non-quota backup option.

— UPCAT Exam Details
📖 Language Proficiency
Vocabulary, grammar, sentence structure, analogies, Filipino language usage (tayutay, salawikain). Tests both English and Filipino proficiency. Approximately 50–60 items.
📚 Reading Comprehension
Passages with questions on main idea, inference, tone, and critical reading. Both English and Filipino texts included. Approximately 50–60 items.
📐 Mathematics
Algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, word problems. Covers the K-12 curriculum up to Grade 12. No calculators allowed. Approximately 60 items.
🧪 Science
Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science. Covers major concepts from Junior and Senior High School. Approximately 60 items.
— What to Bring on Exam Day
🎒
Exam Day Checklist — What to Bring & What NOT to Bring
✅ BRING THESE
  • Printed UPCAT Test Permit — absolutely required, no permit = no entry.
  • At least 2 valid IDs — school ID + government-issued (e.g. PSA birth cert, PRC ID, passport, LTO ID).
  • #2 or HB pencils — at least 3 pencils. Answer sheets are machine-scanned.
  • Black ballpen — for personal details on answer sheets.
  • Eraser (good quality) — for corrections on your answer sheet.
  • Sharpener — small, manual only.
  • Snacks and water — eat before the exam. Some centers allow light snacks.
  • Comfortable clothing — test centers may be cold (aircon). Bring a light jacket.
❌ DO NOT BRING THESE
  • Calculators of any kind — strictly prohibited.
  • Mobile phones / tablets / smartwatches — must be left outside the testing room or fully turned off in bag.
  • Reviewers or notes — no reference materials allowed inside the testing room.
  • Rulers, protractors, or compasses — not allowed.
  • Electronic devices of any type.
  • Extra answer sheets — these are provided by the proctor.
— Test Permit & Admission Slip
🎟️
Test Permit — What It Contains & How to Download It

Your UPCAT Test Permit is your official admission slip for the exam. It is generated after your application is approved and the application period closes. It contains:

  • Your assigned test center — the school or venue where you will take the exam (may be different from your own school).
  • Testing room number — the specific room in the test center.
  • Exam schedule — the date and time of your assigned session (morning or afternoon).
  • Your applicant number — your unique UPCAT ID.
  • Photo and personal information — as submitted in your application.
⚠️ Important Reminders
Print your test permit on white bond paper (A4 or letter size). Do NOT resize, crop, or alter it. Bring 2 copies if possible. Details regarding the availability and release of the UPCAT 2027 test permit will be sent via email once available — test permits are expected to be released in July 2026.
— Typical UPCAT Timeline
March 3–30, 2026: Application Period (Form 1)
The UPCAT 2027 online application window opens. Fill out Form 1 (Application for First Year Admission Personal Data Sheet) at upcat.up.edu.ph using an active email address. The application fee is free for Filipino applicants.
March 3–April 10, 2026: School Confirmation (Form 2A)
The portal forwards a request to your high school principal to submit Form 2A (Senior High School Profile) and confirm your enrollment in Grade 11 or graduation from senior high school. Check your registered email regularly during this period.
July 2026: Test Permit Release
Approved applicants will be notified via email once test permits are available. Log in to the OAS portal to download your UPCAT Test Permit. Print it clearly on white bond paper — do not resize, crop, or alter it.
August 1–2, 2026: UPCAT 2027 Exam Days
The UPCAT 2027 is administered over two days — August 1 and 2, 2026. Arrive 30 minutes early at your assigned test center. The exam typically takes 4–5 hours total across all subtests with short breaks.
August 3–24, 2026: Grades Submission (Form 2B)
Update and resubmit Form 1, then fill out and submit Form 2B (Secondary School Record). The portal notifies your school to submit Form 2A and confirm your Grade 12 enrollment or senior high graduation. Your school also submits a certified copy of your Permanent Secondary School Record (F137/SF10).
January – April (Following Year): Results Release
UPCAT results are released on the official UP Admissions site. Passers are notified with their UPG and campus/program assignment. Enrollment instructions follow shortly after.
— Common Mistakes to Avoid
⚠️
12 Critical Mistakes UPCAT Applicants Should Avoid
  • Submitting incorrect or blurry documents. Your application may be rejected. Use clear, properly formatted scans or photos.
  • Missing the application deadline. There are no extensions. Set multiple calendar reminders for all key dates.
  • Forgetting to pay the application fee. Your application is NOT considered submitted until payment is confirmed.
  • Using a wrong or inactive email address. All notifications come via email — use one you check regularly.
  • Not double-checking your personal information. Errors in your name, birthday, or school info may cause problems when picking up your test permit or on results day.
  • Not printing the test permit properly. Resizing, cropping, or printing on colored paper may cause issues at the test center.
  • Arriving late on exam day. Test centers are strict — latecomers are not admitted. Aim to arrive 30–45 minutes early.
  • Forgetting to bring valid IDs. You need at least 2 valid IDs on exam day alongside your permit.
  • Bringing prohibited items. Calculators, phones, and smartwatches are not allowed. Getting caught can disqualify you.
  • Random guessing without thinking. The UPCAT uses right-minus-wrong scoring — each wrong answer deducts 0.25 points, while blank answers receive no deduction. Always try to eliminate options and make an educated guess, but avoid purely random answers.
  • Not choosing campus/course options strategically. Research quota vs non-quota programs and rank your choices wisely. Put realistic options as backups.
  • Studying only a few weeks before the exam. Start your review at least 3–6 months before exam day for the best results.
— Understanding UPG
🔢
How Is the UPG Computed?

The University Predicted Grade (UPG) is the score used by UP to rank all UPCAT applicants. It combines two components:

  • UPCAT Score (60% weight): Based on your Transmuted Score (TS), which is derived from your raw scores across all 4 subtests (Language Proficiency, Reading Comprehension, Math, Science).
  • High School GWA (40% weight): Your cumulative Grade 11 and (partial) Grade 12 GWA, converted to the UP 1.0–5.0 grade scale.
  • Lower UPG = Better performance. A UPG of 1.0 is the best; 5.0 is the lowest acceptable.
  • Important: The exact UPG formula is not officially published by UP. Always verify with the current official UP Admissions bulletin, as the formula may differ slightly from year to year.

— After the Exam
📊
How to Check Your UPCAT Results
  • Results are released on the official UP System website (up.edu.ph) and individual campus websites.
  • Log in to your OAS account using the same credentials from your application to view your personalized results and UPG.
  • Results show whether you passed, are on the waitlist, or did not pass for each of your chosen programs.
  • Passers receive their UPG and instructions for the enrollment process. Follow the enrollment deadline strictly.
  • Waitlisted applicants may be admitted if enrolled passers do not claim their slots within the given timeframe.
  • Applicants who did not pass may file an appeal for reconsideration within the specified period — check the campus website for the process.
  • Even if you don't pass, explore other pathways into UP — UPCAT-free enrollment programs exist for some units (e.g., UP Open University online courses, transfer student programs).
— FAQs
Is the UPCAT free to take?
The UPCAT application fee is free for Filipino applicants. Check the official UP Admissions site at up.edu.ph for the most current fee details and any applicable conditions.
What are the UPCAT exam materials I must bring?
You must bring: (1) printed UPCAT Test Permit, (2) at least two valid IDs, (3) #2 or HB pencils (at least 3), (4) a black ballpen, and (5) an eraser. Calculators, phones, smartwatches, and all electronic devices are strictly prohibited inside the testing room.
Is there a right-minus-wrong penalty in the UPCAT?
Yes. The UPCAT uses a right-minus-wrong scoring system. Each incorrect answer deducts 0.25 points from your score, while unanswered items receive no deduction. Because of this, examinees are encouraged to make educated guesses — eliminate obviously wrong choices first to improve your odds — rather than leaving items blank or guessing randomly.
How is the UPG calculated?
The University Predicted Grade (UPG) combines your UPCAT Transmuted Score (TS) and your High School GWA. The UPCAT score contributes 60% of the UPG, while your high school GWA contributes the remaining 40%. The lower the UPG, the better.
Can I take the UPCAT more than once?
No. You can only take the UPCAT once in your lifetime. This makes thorough preparation extremely important — every review session counts.
When are UPCAT results released?
Results are typically released 4–6 months after the exam, usually between January and April of the following year. Results are announced via the official UP Admissions website (up.edu.ph) and individual campus sites. Use your OAS credentials to view your personalized results.
Should I enroll in a review center?
Not necessarily. Many UPCAT passers prepared entirely through self-study using free reviewers, practice tests, and online resources. What matters most is consistent, disciplined review over several months. A good reviewer and structured study plan can be just as effective — which is exactly what this vault provides for free.
What if I fail the UPCAT?
There are still pathways into UP: the UP Open University (UPOU) offers degree programs accessible without UPCAT. You may also explore lateral transfer opportunities after completing 1–2 years in another accredited university. Additionally, you can still pursue other excellent Philippine universities — DLSU, AdMU, UST, PUP, and others offer equally strong academic programs.
